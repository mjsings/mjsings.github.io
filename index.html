United Nations Department of Political and Peacebuilding Affairs
Seoul 路 London 路 Paris 路 Bangkok 路 New York

I lead a small team at the UN that deploys AI and emerging technologies for conflict prevention and mediation, with a focus on access and inclusion in high-stakes political contexts.

Since 2020, we've deployed AI-enabled dialogues that brought over 10,000 people into peace processes in low-resource contexts and underserved languages, crafted an open-source satellite platform that makes invisible climate-conflict patterns visible in data-scarce regions, and built training programmes so that UN political analysts can actually work with the technologies reshaping their field.

Before this, I covered Southeast Asian geopolitics from the UN's Bangkok office and spent several years at an innovation think tank working on data and technology policy with EU policymakers. I started out at UNESCO on post-conflict cultural protection programmes and policies (like Timbuktu, Bamiyan, al-Haram al-Sharif).

Most of my career has been spent at the intersection of technology and policy. AI is making it easier than ever for non-technical people to use powerful tools, and I find this most exciting (but also scary) about this moment.

Always happy to chat, especially about ideas for shaping inclusion and access in this age of AI.
